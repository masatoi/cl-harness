### Where the conditions diverge

- 007-error-signal: generic-mcp passed in 9 turns; runtime-native hit
  max-patches at 9 turns.
- 009-handler-case-typo: generic-mcp passed in 7 turns; runtime-native
  hit max-patches.

In both divergent cases **runtime-native lost to generic-mcp**, the
opposite of the original hypothesis. Probe tools didn't help; the
extra freedom seems to cost patch attempts. Increasing
`max-patches` (currently 3) would likely close part of this gap.

### Headline finding

On this single-trial 12-task suite, **`generic-mcp` (83.3%) beats
`runtime-native` (66.7%)** by 2 cells. With clean-verify enabled, both
conditions exhibit:

- ~70-80% pass-rate on local-edit bugs (000-002, 004-006, 010-011).
- Failures cluster on bugs whose fix path requires understanding a
  separate file (003-missing-import) or producing a structurally novel
  form (008-macro-expansion).
- `:max-patches` (default 3) is the dominant failure mode, not
  `:max-turns`. Increasing this budget would likely shift several
  `:limit-exhausted` outcomes to `:passed` at the cost of more wall
  clock and more wasted edits.

### Methodology notes

- A `%sandbox-fixture` correctness bug surfaced during T3.7's first
  pass: cl-mcp worker re-spawns produced colliding `(random)` first
  values, so two consecutive sweeps reused the same sandbox tmpdir;
  combined with `cp -a` preserving stale mtimes, the FASL cache
  silently returned the previous run's patched build. The fixed helper
  now suffixes with `(get-internal-real-time)` and uses plain `cp -r`
  so each task lands in a unique dir with fresh mtimes. The
  runtime-native data here is from the post-fix re-run; the
  generic-mcp data is from the pre-fix run, but spot checks confirm
  every cell shows `verify test-failed` at turn 0 (fixture was
  genuinely broken on entry).
- `:clean-verify-p t` for benchmark mode (T3.8). Numbers above are
  what survived a fresh-worker reverify, not just the dirty-image
  pass-after-patch.
- Token totals are per-call usage from the LLM API.

### Limitations

- **Single trial per cell.** Phase 5.2 variance demo (003 ×
  generic-mcp × n=3 → turns 5/8/20) showed a 4× spread; treat ±20% as
  noise on every number above.
- **No `:file-only` column.** Phase 5.0's 0/4 baseline still stands;
  re-running file-only across all 12 tasks would presumably yield
  ~0/12 and is a low-information use of the budget.
- **Single model.** Other LLMs (gpt-oss-20b on Groq with reasoning,
  gpt-4o-mini, claude-haiku) were not exercised. The runtime-native
  hypothesis may need a model that can use REPL probe results
  productively rather than chase them.
- **Probe-friendly tasks weren't probe-friendly enough.** Tasks 010
  and 011 were *added* in T3.9 to give runtime-native an angle, but
  both conditions passed them. The bugs are still source-readable;
  truly probe-only bugs (e.g. ones that need `(macroexpand-1 ...)`
  or live state inspection to diagnose) are hard to construct.
- **`:max-patches` default 3 is dominant.** Bumping to 5 or 7 would
  likely lift several limit-exhausted cells; that's a tuning
  experiment, not a methodology fix.
